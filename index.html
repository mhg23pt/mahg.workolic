The Trophy

The World Snooker Championship has been held annually since 1927, and has taken place at the iconic Crucible Theatre in Sheffield since 1977. It is the oldest and most prestigious event on the professional circuit, offering the highest ranking points and prize fund. As a pillar of the Triple Crown, it represents the pinnacle of a player’s career and confers legendary status upon its champion. The final, renowned for its technical brilliance and dramatic intensity, attracts millions of spectators around the globe.

Stuart Bingham claimed his first World Snooker Championship title in 2015, at the age of 38, by defeating Shaun Murphy in the final, becoming the oldest British champion since the beginning of the modern era. Mark Selby, known for his methodical play and psychological resilience, lifted the trophy three times in this period: in 2016, overcoming Ding Junhui; in 2017, against John Higgins; and again in 2021, by beating Shaun Murphy. In between these triumphs, Mark Williams returned to the top in 2018, prevailing over John Higgins in a very closely contested final, rekindling his champion status fifteen years after his previous title. In 2019, Judd Trump clinched his first world title by defeating John Higgins, demonstrating precision and consistency, he thus established himself among the best. Ronnie O’Sullivan, nicknamed “The Rocket” for his speed and creativity, lifted the title again in 2020, defeating Kyren Wilson, and repeated the feat in 2022 by overcoming Judd Trump, equalling Stephen Hendry’s record of seven trophies in the modern era. In the three most recent years, the tournament produced maiden champions: in 2023, Luca Brecel became the first Belgian to win the World Snooker Championship, beating Mark Selby in the final and demonstrating the expansion of snooker beyond its traditional British strongholds. In 2024, Kyren Wilson secured his first World Snooker Championship title after an intense final against Mark Selby, establishing himself as one of the leading players of the new generation. Just yesterday, the 2025 World Championship concluded with an unprecedented winner. Zhao Xintong, the 28-year-old Chinese player, made history for his country by defeating Mark Williams 18–12 in the final. In doing so, he became the first Asian—and indeed the first non-European—player to win snooker’s world championship. In summary, the last decade has showcased both the continuity of established legends such as Selby and O’Sullivan and the emergence of new champions from outside the United Kingdom, bearing witness to the globalisation and high competitive standard of professional snooker.


Triple-Crown

The Triple Crown in snooker comprises the three most prestigious professional tournaments, winning all three titles at any point in a career is considered the ultimate mark of snooker excellence.

  1. World Championship - Held annually since 1927 at the Crucible Theatre in Sheffield.

  2. UK Championship - Inaugurated in 1977, it is the second most important ranking event, steeped in British tradition.

  3. The Masters - An invitational tournament without ranking points, staged since 1975 and featuring the world’s top 16 players.
